How to Export Your ChatGPT Conversations

Follow these steps once to set up your export button. After that, one click is all it takes.

📋 Before you start

You'll need Safari, Chrome, or Edge for Mac and you need to be able to log into chatgpt.com. That's it — no downloads, no software to install.

One-time setup

Get your export button ready

You only need to do this once. After these steps, exporting your conversations is a single click.

1

Open this Setup Page in your browser

Make sure you are reading this guide in Safari, Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge for Mac, or Brave.

If you are reading this in a different app — like Preview or another viewer — right-click the get-my-chatgpt-data.html file and choose Open With → Safari (or Chrome).

ℹ️ On most Macs, Safari is the default browser — it looks like a blue compass in your Dock at the bottom of the screen.
2

Show your Favourites / Bookmarks Bar

The bookmarks bar (called the "Favourites Bar" in Safari) is the row of saved links just below the address bar. We need it visible.

In Safari: Click the word View in the menu bar at the very top of your screen, then click Show Favourites Bar.

3

Open the Setup Page

Your IT team has provided a file called get-my-chatgpt-data.html. Open it now by double-clicking it. It will open as a web page in your browser.

You should see a green button that says "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data".

ℹ️ The file was probably placed in your Downloads folder or sent to you by IT. To find your Downloads folder, click the Finder icon in your Dock (it looks like a blue and white smiley face), then click Downloads in the left sidebar.
4

Drag the green button to your Favourites Bar

This is the key step. You are going to click and hold the green button on that page, then drag it up to the favourites bar and let go.

  1. Move your cursor over the green "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data" button.
  2. Press and hold the trackpad or mouse button down.
  3. While still holding, drag upward toward the favourites bar at the top.
  4. When you see your cursor hovering over the favourites bar, let go.

Every time you export

Export your conversations

The setup above is done. From now on, these two steps are all you need.

5

Go to ChatGPT and log in

Open a new tab and go to chatgpt.com. Log in with your work account — the same username and password (or Okta/SSO) you use for other work tools.

You just need to be on the ChatGPT website. You don't need to open a specific conversation.

ℹ️ Already logged in and ChatGPT is open? You can skip straight to the next step.
6

Click your "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data" bookmark

While you are on chatgpt.com, look at your favourites bar and click the button you added in Step 4.

A progress window will appear in the middle of your screen:

📥
Exporting your ChatGPT conversations
Exported 23 / 90…
Please keep this tab open

Just wait. Do not close the tab or switch off your Mac. The progress bar fills up as it downloads your conversations.

When it finishes you will see a ✅ Done! message and a ZIP file will automatically save to your Downloads folder.

💡 If you have many conversations, this can take several minutes. The screen may look like nothing is happening — that's normal, it's working in the background. You can see other apps while you wait.
7

Find your exported file

When done, a file called something like chatgpt-export-2026-05-28.zip will be in your Downloads folder.

To open it:

  1. Click the Finder icon in your Dock (blue and white smiley face)
  2. Click Downloads in the left sidebar
  3. Find the file starting with chatgpt-export-
  4. Double-click it to unzip it — a folder will appear

Inside you'll find your conversations as JSON (raw data) and Markdown (readable text) files.

ℹ️ You can also find recent downloads quickly by looking at the download indicator in your browser toolbar — it usually looks like an arrow pointing down ↓ or a small circle.
🎉

You're done!

Your ChatGPT conversations have been exported. Next time, just go to chatgpt.com and click your bookmark — no setup needed.

❓ Common questions

The button disappeared from my favourites bar

Go back to the get-my-chatgpt-data.html file and drag the green button to your favourites bar again (Step 4). Bookmarks can sometimes get accidentally removed.

I get an error saying I'm not logged in

The export only works when you are on the chatgpt.com website and logged in. Make sure the address bar says chatgpt.com before clicking the bookmark. If you're on a different site or not logged in, it won't work.

A progress bar appeared but then showed an error message

Your ChatGPT session probably expired. Refresh the chatgpt.com page, log in again if needed, and then click the bookmark once more.

I can't find the ZIP file in Downloads

Open Finder (blue smiley face in your Dock) and click Downloads in the left sidebar. The file will be named chatgpt-export- followed by today's date. If it's not there, check your browser's download history by clicking the ↓ arrow in the toolbar.

Safari won't let me drag the button to the favourites bar

Try the right-click method instead: right-click (two-finger tap on trackpad) the green button → "Add Link to Bookmarks" → change the location to Favourites → Add. Alternatively, use Chrome or Edge instead of Safari for this step — it works more reliably on those browsers.

Can I export more than once?

Yes — every time you click the bookmark it creates a new export with today's date in the filename. Your previous export files stay in Downloads alongside the new one.

The export is taking a very long time

If you have many conversations, the export can take several minutes. Keep the ChatGPT tab open and don't close your browser. The progress bar will keep moving as long as it is still working. You can use other applications on your Mac while you wait.

Does this work in Brave browser?

Yes — Brave is supported on macOS. Follow the same steps as Chrome.

My Mac went to sleep — did the export stop?

Possibly. Wake your Mac up and check if the progress window is still showing. If it shows an error, just click the bookmark again to start a fresh export — it won't create duplicates, the new file will just have today's date.
