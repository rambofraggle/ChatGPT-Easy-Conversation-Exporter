How to Export Your ChatGPT Conversations

Follow these steps once to set up your export button. After that, one click is all it takes.

📋 Before you start

You'll need Microsoft Edge or Google Chrome installed on your computer, and you need to be able to log into chatgpt.com. That's it — no downloads, no software to install.

One-time setup

Get your export button ready

You only need to do this once. After these steps, exporting your conversations is a single click.

1

Open this Setup Page in your browser

Make sure you are reading this guide in Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, or Brave. (Most company computers use Edge by default.)

If you are reading this in a different app — like a PDF viewer or a different browser — right-click the file and choose Open with → Microsoft Edge.

ℹ️ Not sure which browser you are using? Look at the icon in your taskbar at the bottom of the screen. Edge looks like a blue wave 𝑒, Chrome looks like a coloured circle, and Brave looks like an orange and white lion.
2

Show your Bookmarks Bar

The bookmarks bar is the row of saved links just below the address bar at the top of your browser. We need it visible.

Press this keyboard shortcut:

Ctrl + Shift + B

A row of buttons should appear just below the address bar.

3

Open the Setup Page

Your IT team has provided a file called get-my-chatgpt-data.html. Open it now by double-clicking it. It will open as a web page in your browser.

You should see a green button that says "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data".

ℹ️ The file was probably placed in your Downloads folder or sent to you by IT. If you can't find it, ask your IT support contact.
4

Drag the green button to your Bookmarks Bar

This is the key step. You are going to click and hold the green button on that page, then drag it up to the bookmarks bar and let go.

  1. Move your mouse over the green "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data" button.
  2. Press and hold the left mouse button down.
  3. While still holding, drag your mouse up toward the bookmarks bar.
  4. When you see your cursor hovering over the bookmarks bar, let go.

Every time you export

Export your conversations

The setup above is done. From now on, these two steps are all you need to do.

5

Go to ChatGPT and log in

Open a new tab and go to chatgpt.com. Log in with your work account (your company's SSO/Okta login, the same one you use for other work tools).

You only need to be on the ChatGPT website — you don't need to open a specific conversation.

ℹ️ If you are already logged in and ChatGPT is open, you can skip straight to the next step.
6

Click your "⬇️ Export My ChatGPT Data" bookmark

While you are on chatgpt.com, look at your bookmarks bar and click the button you added in Step 4.

A progress window will appear in the middle of the screen showing you how it's going:

📥
Exporting your ChatGPT conversations
Exported 47 / 150…
Please keep this tab open

Just wait. Do not close the tab. The progress bar will fill up as your conversations are downloaded.

When it finishes, you will see a ✅ Done! message and a ZIP file will automatically download.

💡 If you have a lot of conversations (hundreds), this can take a few minutes. Your computer screen may look like nothing is happening — that's fine, it's working in the background.
7

Find your exported file

When the export is complete, a file called something like chatgpt-export-2026-05-28.zip will be in your Downloads folder.

To open your Downloads folder:

  1. Press Windows + E to open File Explorer
  2. Click Downloads in the left panel
  3. Look for the file starting with chatgpt-export-

Inside the ZIP you will find your conversations saved as JSON (raw data) and Markdown (readable text files).

ℹ️ You can also find recent downloads by looking at the download bar at the top-right corner of your browser (the arrow ↓ icon).
🎉

That's it — you're done!

Your ChatGPT conversations have been exported. The next time you need to export, just go to chatgpt.com and click your bookmark — no setup needed.

❓ Common questions

The green button disappeared from my bookmarks bar

Go back to the get-my-chatgpt-data.html file and drag the button to your bookmarks bar again (Step 4). Bookmarks can sometimes get accidentally deleted.

I get an error saying I'm not logged in

The export only works when you are on the chatgpt.com website and logged in. Make sure the address bar says chatgpt.com, not some other website, before clicking the bookmark.

The progress bar appeared but then showed an error

This usually means your ChatGPT session expired. Refresh the chatgpt.com page, log in again, and then click the bookmark once more.

I can't find the exported ZIP file

Press Windows + E to open File Explorer, then click Downloads on the left side. The file will be named something like chatgpt-export-2026-05-28.zip. If it's not there, check that your browser didn't redirect downloads to a different folder — click the ↓ icon in your browser toolbar to see recent downloads.

Can I run this export more than once?

Yes — every time you click the bookmark it creates a fresh export with today's date in the filename. Your old export files stay in Downloads alongside the new one.

Does this work in Brave browser?

Yes — Brave is supported on Windows. Follow the same steps as Chrome.

It's taking a really long time

If you have many conversations, the export can take several minutes. Keep the ChatGPT tab open and don't close the browser. The progress bar will keep moving as long as it's working.
